\section{Tail Bounds Framework}


The lecture introduces fundamental probabilistic tools used to bound extreme deviations.


\subsection{Markov’s Inequality}


A general inequality used to bound the probability that a non-negative random variable deviates far from its expectation.


\subsection{Chebyshev’s Inequality}


A refinement over Markov’s inequality that incorporates variance to provide tighter bounds on deviations from the mean.


\subsection{Chernoff Bound}


A stronger exponential bound used for analyzing the tail probabilities of sums of random variables, particularly useful in high-dimensional and large-scale systems.
